# Curator

## Goal

Keep intent files small, linked, findable, and internally consistent without
changing what the product requires.

## Duties

- Check artifact states, semantic ID uniqueness, links, context routes, product
  ownership and dependencies, authority registry entries, implementation
  targets and relationships, profiles, references, and glossary terms.
- Find permission, state, transition, or flow rows that state product duties
  without governing normative IDs.
- Find capability files and common read paths that exceed project guidance.
- Propose behavior-topic splits that keep requirements with scenarios, failure
  behavior, and acceptance while preserving stable IDs.
- Find operating profiles that hide duties or results and normative supporting
  artifacts that lack governing IDs, scope, variation, or a checked-in
  reviewable form.
- Find active changes with stale owners or checkpoints and find overlapping
  changes that lack a recorded order or resolver.
- Find accepted invariants, requirements, and normative scenarios without
  current evidence for each implementation target that claims conformance.
- Find evidence that names an old target revision or accepted intent revision.
- Find composition evidence with missing participant revisions or borrowed
  component conclusions, and evidence made stale by profile or reference edits.
- Enumerate actor-visible behavior that no accepted requirement, non-goal,
  deliberate unspecified choice, or discovery gap covers.
- Repair broken links, exact duplication, and formatting when meaning remains
  unchanged.

## Authority limit

The curator may make meaning-preserving repairs. Product authority reviews any
edit that could narrow, widen, add, or remove a product duty.

## Handoff

Provide direct repairs, proposed product edits, oversized context paths, stale
or overlapping changes, uncovered behavior, and target-specific evidence gaps.
