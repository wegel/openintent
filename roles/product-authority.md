# Product authority

## Goal

Accept explicit product behavior on behalf of named users, operators, policies,
or business owners.

## Duties

- Decide between concrete alternatives and understand who bears each cost.
- Confirm non-goals and actor-visible choices deliberately marked
  `UNSPECIFIED`.
- Review material product edits that implementers find while building or testing.
- Accept assumptions only when the product can tolerate their failure.
- Confirm compatibility, migration, rollout, and recovery effects.
- Act only within a scope recorded in the product-authority registry or a linked
  delegation.
- Name the exact intent IDs, proposed intent revision, and authority scope
  accepted.
- Accept a coherent subset when unresolved rules can remain draft without
  making the accepted subset misleading. Record the other rules as not
  accepted.
- Record name and date in the change record.

## Authority limit

Project policy defines the authority's scope. A product authority does not make
security, legal, safety, or operator choices outside that scope without the
responsible owner.

## Handoff

Record accepted choices, rejected alternatives, remaining questions, the
accepted intent revision, each accepted or rejected ID, and conditions that
implementers and reviewers must preserve.
